Dr. Patel: What makes you say that? The researchers used a range of methodologies, including RNA-FISH and immunofluorescence.

Dr. Patel: I see your point. However, the consistency across their samples suggests a strong likelihood of accuracy. It's not often we see such coherence in cellular localization studies.

Dr. Chen: I agree, but have you considered potential cross-reactivity in their antibodies? That could skew the results.

Dr. Patel: Fair enough. But even if there's some cross-reactivity, would it be significant enough to invalidate their findings? The RNA-FISH results were quite clear.

Dr. Chen: Perhaps not invalidate, but it does cast some doubt. Also, they didn't fully explore the functional implications of Linc00173 in macrophage nuclei. That's a critical gap.

Dr. Patel: That's a valid critique. Understanding its function could indeed solidify the claim. But isn't it premature to dismiss their findings entirely?

Dr. Chen: I'm not dismissing them, just questioning. We need more comprehensive studies. Replication by other labs would strengthen their argument.

Dr. Patel: Agreed. I think further investigation is warranted, especially with larger samples and different methodologies. But this discovery could be a significant stepping stone.

Dr. Chen: Definitely, and it'll be exciting to see how this unfolds. Perhaps we could propose a follow-up study to address these gaps?
