
 Scientific claim: Understanding epigenetic regulation of replication is essential for the reational design of episomally replicating vectors. 
 Participant Dynamics: Advocate vs. Skeptic 
 Contextual Arena: The Didactic Arena (goal: to teach or learn) 
 Interaction Trigger: An External Mandate (a new law, policy, or trend) 
 Dialogue Objective: To Define the Disagreement (map out points of conflict) 
```
Advocate: With the new research funding mandate focusing on genetic vector design, we must emphasize understanding epigenetic regulation of replication. It's essential for the rational design of episomally replicating vectors.

Skeptic: I'm not convinced that deep-diving into epigenetics is the best use of resources right now. Isn't vector design complicated enough without adding another layer?

Advocate: Well, the complexity is precisely why it's crucial. Epigenetic factors can dramatically influence vector stability and expression. Understanding them can lead to more efficient and reliable applications.

Skeptic: But isn’t the current focus on genomic sequences already providing tangible results? I mean, why shift the priority when the existing approach works?

Advocate: Because the existing approach has limitations. Think about it—epigenetic regulation could improve vector persistence and control over gene expression without genomic integration. It's about precision.

Skeptic: Precision is important, sure. But doesn’t this make the process more susceptible to unforeseen complications? More variables mean more room for error.

Advocate: Not necessarily. With proper understanding, epigenetic regulation can actually reduce errors by providing additional control mechanisms. And it aligns with the new policy aiming for innovation in gene therapy.

Skeptic: I see your point, but isn’t there a risk of overcomplicating the research pipeline? We have to ensure the end goal remains practical and achievable.

Advocate: True, but ignoring these factors might leave us trailing behind in advancements. The policy is about staying at the forefront of science. Epigenetics could be the key to unlocking new potentials in vector design.

Skeptic: I suppose there's merit in exploring it further, but cautiously. We need to balance ambition with feasibility.

Advocate: Absolutely, balance is key. But we can't afford to overlook the potential benefits. If done right, it could revolutionize how we approach gene therapy.

Skeptic: Agreed, albeit with some reservations. Let’s ensure the discourse remains open as we move forward.

Advocate: Definitely. Open dialogue will be crucial as we navigate these new scientific frontiers.
```
