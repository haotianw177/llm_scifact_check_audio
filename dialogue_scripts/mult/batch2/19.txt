Speaker 2: Fascinating. And this has implications for the new mandate on research funding for aging studies. Seems like a good time to explore if this claim holds water.

Speaker 2: Yes, I have. But here’s the thing, the data primarily relies on associative learning tests. It's a bit like measuring intelligence with just one type of exam, isn't it?

Speaker 2: Precisely. Plus, learning capacity isn’t a static measure. Could it be that these 7-day-old worms are learning differently, not less?

Speaker 1: That's an interesting perspective. You're saying we might be misinterpreting different learning expressions as diminished capacity?

Speaker 2: Exactly. Add to that the fact that the funding policy is moving based on potentially incomplete data. We need to ensure our conclusions are robust before redirecting resources.

Speaker 1: So, we're looking at a flaw in the interpretation of the data, which could mislead policy. This warrants deeper investigation, doesn't it?

Speaker 2: Certainly. Before we change the course of research funding, we need a comprehensive understanding. Otherwise, we risk undermining the very science we aim to support.

Speaker 1: Agreed. Let's advocate for a more nuanced approach in this debate. 

Speaker 2: Absolutely. The truth must guide our actions.
