Our studies indicate that, despite cell line differences, the core mechanisms of differentiation involve synchronized changes. It’s not about oversimplification but recognizing a consistent biological theme.

**Dr. Mitchell:** But isn't it a leap to generalize this pattern across all embryonic stem cells? The cost of error here could be significant, especially in clinical contexts. We need to expose any potential flaws before moving forward.

**Dr. Lewis:** Precisely why ongoing research and peer review are essential. We aim to validate these findings robustly. The goal is to enhance our understanding, not to prematurely conclude.

**Dr. Mitchell:** I appreciate your dedication to rigor. Let’s ensure that in our pursuit of truth, we remain vigilant about the nuances. Perhaps a deeper dive into the exceptions could reveal more truths about the rule.

**Dr. Lewis:** Agreed. Collaboration and skepticism drive scientific progress. Let’s continue this dialogue with a focus on refining and validating our claims.

**Dr. Mitchell:** Absolutely, Dr. Lewis. It’s through these discussions that we edge closer to the truth.
