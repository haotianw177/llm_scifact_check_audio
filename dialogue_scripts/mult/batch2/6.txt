
 Scientific claim: 10% of sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS) deaths happen in newborns aged less than 6 months. 
 Participant Dynamics: Peer vs. Peer 
 Contextual Arena: The Persuasive Arena (goal: to win over an audience) 
 Interaction Trigger: An External Mandate (a new law, policy, or trend) 
 Dialogue Objective: To Define the Disagreement (map out points of conflict) 
```
Speaker 1: So, the new policy mandates that all hospitals implement stricter guidelines for monitoring newborns to reduce SIDS. But is that really necessary when only 10% of SIDS cases occur in infants less than six months old?

Speaker 2: Well, the policy aims to address the most vulnerable age group, even if it's just 10%. That's still hundreds of lives potentially saved each year.

Speaker 1: True, but the focus seems disproportionate. Shouldn't resources be allocated more evenly across all age groups affected by SIDS?

Speaker 2: I see your point, but consider the correlation between age and risk factors. Infants under six months are more susceptible due to their developmental stage. It makes sense to prioritize them.

Speaker 1: But what about the cost implications? Hospitals will need more staff and equipment, which could strain budgets and affect care quality in other areas.

Speaker 2: That's a valid concern. Yet, if early intervention can prevent SIDS, the long-term savings in healthcare costs and emotional toll on families could outweigh the initial expenses.

Speaker 1: Okay, but how can we ensure that these guidelines are effective? There's no guarantee that increased monitoring will directly lead to a decrease in SIDS cases.

Speaker 2: No system is foolproof, but the guidelines are based on the latest research and expert recommendations. They're our best bet for now.

Speaker 1: I guess my main worry is that we're focusing too much on one aspect of a complex issue. SIDS isn't solely about age; there are multiple factors at play.

Speaker 2: Absolutely, and that's why public education about safe sleeping practices and ongoing research is also crucial. The policy isn't a silver bullet, but a piece of a larger puzzle.

Speaker 1: Fair enough. Let's hope these measures lead to better outcomes without unintended consequences elsewhere.

Speaker 2: Agreed. It's about finding the right balance to protect our most vulnerable.
```
