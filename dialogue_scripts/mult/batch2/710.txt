 Participant Dynamics: Practitioner vs. Decision-Maker

```plaintext
Practitioner: Good afternoon, Dr. Lee. I wanted to discuss a recent discovery that could impact our asthma treatment protocols.

Decision-Maker: Afternoon, Dr. Patel. Please, go on. I'm intrigued.

Practitioner: So, a new study suggests that long chain polyunsaturated fatty acids supplementation can significantly reduce wheezing and asthma symptoms.

Decision-Maker: That's quite a claim. What's the science behind it?

Practitioner: The study indicates these fatty acids, particularly omega-3s, help reduce inflammation in the airways. It seems they modulate the immune response and decrease the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines.

Decision-Maker: Interesting. But how robust is this study? Were the results statistically significant?

Practitioner: Yes, they were. The study was a double-blind, placebo-controlled trial involving over 300 participants. The results showed a marked improvement in respiratory function.

Decision-Maker: That's promising. But how does this fit within our current treatment landscape? Are there any side effects we should be concerned about?

Practitioner: The side effects are minimal, mainly digestive discomfort, but they were rare. It could be a complementary approach, especially for patients who don't respond well to steroids.

Decision-Maker: It's essential to empower our patients with options. But should we consider this as an adjunct or a primary treatment?

Practitioner: I would recommend it as an adjunct for now. More studies will help us understand its full potential. But it offers a natural alternative that many patients might prefer.

Decision-Maker: I see. We should certainly consider this as part of our patient discussions. How soon can we incorporate this into our recommendations?

Practitioner: We could start with a pilot program within the next quarter. Educating our team will be crucial to ensure informed discussions with patients.

Decision-Maker: Agreed, Dr. Patel. Let's prepare a proposal and present it at our next strategy meeting. Empowering patients with knowledge and choice is a priority.

Practitioner: Absolutely, Dr. Lee. I'll get started on the documentation right away.
```
