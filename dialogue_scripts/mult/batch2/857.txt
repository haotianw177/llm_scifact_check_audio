 Participant Dynamics: Peer vs. Peer 
 Contextual Arena: The Personal Arena (goal: to resolve a personal, high-stakes matter) 
 Interaction Trigger: An External Mandate (a new law, policy, or trend) 
 Dialogue Objective: To Reach a Decision (choose or reject an action) 
```   
Speaker 1: So, have you heard about the new research on NSAIDs? Apparently, they’re being looked at for their potential anticancer properties.

Speaker 2: Yeah, I read the article yesterday. It’s intriguing, isn't it? But the timing is a bit tricky with the new healthcare policy coming in.

Speaker 1: Exactly. This new policy emphasizes cost-effective treatment options. If NSAIDs show real promise, they could be a game-changer for cancer therapy costs.

Speaker 2: That’s true. But don’t you think we’re jumping the gun? The research is still in early stages. What if we recommend them and it turns out they’re not effective or worse, harmful in the long run?

Speaker 1: That’s a valid concern. But we also have to consider that the earlier we adopt potentially beneficial treatments, the more lives we might save. It’s a tough call.

Speaker 2: I agree. I just worry about the liability if something goes wrong. Remember the last time we rushed a treatment to market without sufficient evidence?

Speaker 1: Yes, that was a fiasco. But we also can’t ignore the mandate pushing for innovative solutions. We have to be proactive. Maybe a controlled trial within our clinic first?

Speaker 2: That could work. It’s a safer approach. We can monitor the outcomes closely before making any broader recommendations. It might satisfy the policy requirements too.

Speaker 1: Precisely. And if we document everything meticulously, we’d have a solid case for either continuing or halting the use based on real evidence.

Speaker 2: Okay, let’s draft a proposal for a small-scale trial. We’ll present it at the next board meeting and see if we can get approval.

Speaker 1: Sounds like a plan. Let’s ensure we’re thorough with the data collection. We owe it to the patients and our practice to make informed decisions.

Speaker 2: Agreed. Let’s get started on that proposal.
```
