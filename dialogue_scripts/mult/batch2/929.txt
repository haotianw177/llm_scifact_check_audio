Dr. Shaw: Promising, yes. But without replication and peer validation, we’re jumping the gun. You can’t present this as a breakthrough just yet.

Dr. Shaw: I’m not dismissing the potential impact, Ellis. But we need to maintain scientific rigor. If we push this without concrete evidence, we risk losing credibility.

Dr. Ellis: I agree. Yet, shouldn’t we be exploring every potential lead? This could lead to developing preventative strategies.

Dr. Shaw: Absolutely, exploration is key. But we should focus on verifying the findings first. Let’s organize a replication study. If it holds, then we can truly celebrate.

Dr. Ellis: That’s a fair approach. Let’s gather a team and design a follow-up study. If this holds true, it could be a game-changer.
